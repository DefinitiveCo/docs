QuickTrade is an execution mode for **market orders** that optimizes for speed of execution over price. It is designed for entering fast-moving markets, such as sniping newly launched tokens, where landing the fill quickly matters more than getting the tightest possible price.

You enable it with a single flag on the order request:

```jsonc
{
  "targetChain": "base",
  "contraChain": "base",
  "targetAsset": "0x4200000000000000000000000000000000000006", // WETH
  "contraAsset": "0x833589fCD6eDb6E08f4c7C32D4f71b54bdA02913", // USDC
  "side": "buy",
  "qty": "100",
  "orderType": "market",
  "quickTrade": true
}
```

## Why it is fast

A normal market order fetches a fresh quote at submission time, then simulates and routes it before execution. QuickTrade removes that round trip:

- **Reusable quotes.** QuickTrade executes against a recently computed quote instead of fetching a new one. Quotes are pre-populated by the quote/preview endpoint (which clients typically call in the background) and reused for a short window, so a submit can skip straight to execution. Under the hood the cached quote's calldata is rewritten to your exact amount.
- **Prioritized execution.** QuickTrade tolerates more slippage and prioritizes gas so the transaction lands quickly and reliably in competitive blocks, rather than retrying its way in.

The result is meaningfully lower submit-to-execution latency, at the cost of the price trade-offs described below.

## When to use it

Use QuickTrade when time-to-fill is the priority:

- Entering a newly launched token where the market is moving within seconds.
- Any market buy or sell where a certain, fast fill is worth more than shaving basis points off the price.

Use a standard market order (omit `quickTrade`) when you want the tightest price and can tolerate normal quoting latency.

## Trade-offs

QuickTrade optimizes for landing the fill, so you accept:

- **A wider effective price.** The wider slippage schedule tolerates more slippage to avoid slippage-based retries. Your `maxSlippage` and `maxPriceImpact` bounds still apply — if the fill cannot meet them, no funds move.
- **Higher gas.** QuickTrade prioritizes gas to land the transaction faster, so gas costs are higher than a standard market order.
- **A slightly older quote.** Because execution reuses a recent quote rather than pulling a fresh one, the reference price can be marginally staler than a standard order.

## Constraints

| Rule | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Market orders only | `quickTrade` is rejected on any non-market order type (`limit`, `twap`, `stop`, `stop-loss`, `take-profit`). |
| Shorter working window | QuickTrade market orders use a shorter default expiry than standard market orders, matching the snipe-now-or-skip intent. |

## Field reference

| Field | Type | Applies to | Meaning |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| `quickTrade` | boolean | Market orders only | Enable low-latency execution mode. Defaults to `false`. |

<Tip>
  Pre-warm the quote path for the assets you plan to trade by requesting quotes ahead of time. QuickTrade reuses those recent quotes, so a quote already in hand is what makes the submit fast.
</Tip>
